Smoothing and Market Value Reductions (MVR)

To try and deliver steady returns, the With Profits Fund applies a mechanism called smoothing. This means we may hold back some of your returns when market performance is strong, in order to reduce the impact of periods of poor market returns.

If you choose to withdraw money from your ISA at certain times (usually when share prices have fallen substantially), we may also apply an adjustment which reduces how much you get back from that withdrawal. This is called a Market Value Reduction.

A Market Value Reduction is not a charge applied for Wesleyan's benefit. Itâ€™s designed to protect those still invested in the fund from a high volume of withdrawals being made by other customers at unfairly high valuations.

How it works

When you save money into your ISA, you buy units in our With Profits Fund. The fund aims to provide capital growth over a medium to long-term period, investing in UK and international shares, government and corporate debt, property, cash and other financial investments (you can take a look at our With Profits ISA factsheet (PDF 200kb) to find out more about how the fund invests). Crucially, our With Profits Fund differs from other investment funds in two key respects - profit sharing and smoothing:

01

Share in our success

The value of the With Profits Fund depends not just on returns from our investments, but also on Wesleyanâ€™s profits and losses from across the business. As a mutual, we have no shareholders to pay - so we can share our financial successes with you. We do this where possible by adding regular bonuses to your ISA throughout the period of your investment. Sometimes, you might be entitled to a final bonus too. We canâ€™t promise to pay bonuses, but when we do add them, they are guaranteed (subject to any Market Value Reduction (MVR) in place) - and can make a significant difference to your final cash-in value.

02

Smoother, steadier returns

While all investments are subject to the volatility of the stock market, our fund seeks to reduce sharp rises and falls through a mechanism called smoothing. This means holding back some returns when market performance is strong, to support returns when the market experiences losses - mitigating the effect of any short-term market fluctuations. Before you apply for your ISA, please make sure you understand how smoothing, bonuses and Market Value Reductions (MVRs) work. They are discussed in the How our With Profits Fund works (PDF 200kb) booklet.
